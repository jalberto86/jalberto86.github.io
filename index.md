**Teaching and Research Fellow** — Université d’Evry–Paris-Saclay (CEPS), Palaiseau, France  
**Former Marie Curie / APOGEEBIO postdoctoral researcher** — Institut Mines-Télécom Business School (LITEM)

**Summary.** **Teaching:** I teach microeconomics, econometrics, and statistics with a step-by-step approach focused on economic intuition, methodological rigor, and quantitative skills. 

**Research:** My work sits at the intersection of environmental and energy economics, technological change, and international trade. I study how climate and trade policies reshape incentives to innovate and how these changes propagate through global value chains, land use, and GHG emissions, drawing in particular on CGE/GTAP modelling (GAMS), applied econometrics, and innovation network analysis (patents). 

**Applied economics:** Part of my background is rooted in policy-oriented analysis and evaluation for public and international institutions, including work on innovation/productivity indicators and energy and trade policy issues, with a consistent focus on connecting research outputs to operational needs and public policy choices.

**Research interests:** environmental & energy economics; technological change and innovation; biofuels & net-zero pathways; CGE/GTAP modelling; trade and environmental impacts; land-use change and GHG emissions.
